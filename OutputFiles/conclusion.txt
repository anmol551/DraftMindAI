1. CONCLUSION
----------------------------------------
The research focuses on an automated skin lesion classification pipeline using the HAM10000 dataset, covering seven distinct categories. This system begins with raw images and metadata, conducting thorough data analysis to understand class distribution and image properties before processing. A detailed preprocessing approach includes hair removal via DullRazor, colour adjustments per channel, and contrast enhancement through CLAHE to ensure clean input for model training. Data splitting at the lesion level avoids overlap across training, validation, and testing sets, while a sampling method balances the dominant class disparity. Four models, namely InceptionV3, EfficientNet-B0, Xception, and ConvNeXt-Tiny, undergo training with consistent settings, using pretrained weights and specific optimisation techniques. Among these, ConvNeXt-Tiny stands out with an accuracy of 77.34%, a weighted F1 score of 78.29%, and a melanoma recall of 58.58%, surpassing other architectures by notable margins. The innovation in this work lies in combining a multi-step preprocessing sequence with a modern convolutional design, ensuring artefacts are minimised before any further image adjustments during training. This structured approach differs from earlier studies by prioritising noise reduction early in the pipeline. At the model level, ConvNeXt-Tiny incorporates unique design elements like intermediate layers and dropout to improve stability and reduce overfitting on smaller classes. Compared to a baseline model like InceptionV3, this pipeline shows a gain of 10.76% in accuracy, alongside better detection rates for critical cases. Beyond core classification, the trained ConvNeXt-Tiny model integrates into a web application built with Flask, enabling users to upload images for instant analysis. This tool displays probability scores for each category and issues alerts for high-risk predictions above a set threshold, bridging the gap between technical development and practical use in dermatoscopic analysis.

2. FUTURE WORK
----------------------------------------
Future Work and Recommendations

Exploring new paths for research in skin lesion classification can improve current approaches. A key area is testing different machine learning and deep learning structures beyond models like ConvNeXt-Tiny, which showed a top accuracy of 77.34%. Investigating other designs could reveal better ways to handle complex image patterns. Bringing in varied data sources, such as patient history or environmental factors, might also strengthen model predictions. Refining how features are chosen can make results easier to understand for medical professionals. Adopting more detailed testing methods could ensure models perform well across diverse scenarios, building trust in their outcomes.

Another direction involves examining how preprocessing steps affect model success. Current methods like hair removal and colour normalisation have shown value, but comparing other techniques may yield improved clarity in images. Applying these models to real-world settings, such as clinical environments or telemedicine platforms, remains a vital step. This would test their practical use in supporting doctors with diagnosis. Addressing ethical concerns and ensuring fairness in predictions is equally important. Models must avoid bias against certain skin types or demographics to maintain equitable healthcare delivery.

Research could also focus on blending multiple data types for a fuller picture of skin conditions. Combining image analysis with other diagnostic tools might enhance accuracy beyond the current top micro-AUC of 0.9665 seen in ConvNeXt-Tiny. Exploring how to balance datasets, given the severe imbalance noted in class distribution, could prevent skewed results. Developing ways to explain model decisions to non-experts would aid in building confidence among users. Future efforts should aim to bridge gaps between technical advancements and practical application, ensuring that such tools meet real needs in medical settings while upholding high standards of fairness and clarity in their operation.
