4. FAILED ATTEMPTS
----------------------------------------
Introduction to Failed Attempts
This section explores unsuccessful approaches in the research on predictive modelling for financial risk assessment. It aims to shed light on why certain methods did not meet expectations during the study.

Early Design Challenges
An initial attempt with a balancing technique for class imbalance proved unsuitable. It distorted the continuous nature of the risk score target, leading to unreliable outcomes for regression tasks.

Standalone Classification Pitfall
Building separate binary classifiers for loan approval was considered but dismissed. This approach risked creating inconsistency between risk scores and decisions, reducing the clarity of the system.

Underperforming Linear Model
A linear model was tested but showed limited capability with an R2 score of 73.88% and accuracy of 82.73%. It struggled to capture complex patterns in the data.

5. RESEARCH NOVELTY
----------------------------------------
Novel Contributions of the Research

This study brings fresh ideas to the field of financial risk assessment through a unique design that combines continuous risk scoring and binary loan approval into one streamlined process. This approach avoids the need for separate models and ensures that risk estimates and approval decisions align closely. A single TabNet model is used with its output split at the median to make yes or no decisions while keeping the depth of the risk score intact.

A key aspect of this work is the use of TabNet's attention-driven method for picking important features step by step. This offers clear insight into which factors matter most unlike typical neural networks. Such transparency is vital in finance where rules demand clarity. The training setup also uses a special batch strategy to improve performance on structured data without the usual issues of small batch sizes.

Another distinct feature is the creation of six new financial measures. One such measure, the Debt Stress Index, blends debt-to-income ratio with interest rate to reflect real-world stress. These crafted indicators embed expert knowledge directly into the data reducing the model's workload compared to using raw figures alone.

The research also adds a policy layer after the model's predictions. This step applies strict lending rules to override high-risk outputs ensuring decisions meet institutional standards. This blend of data-driven results and rule-based checks is a practical design not often seen in existing studies.

Lastly, the use of a language model to turn numerical results into detailed written reports stands out. This method creates clear explanations from complex scores aiding in reviews and communication with stakeholders. These innovations mark this study as a significant step forward in blending technology with practical financial needs.
