The notebook implements a dual-task financial risk prediction pipeline using a synthetic loan dataset of 20,000 records. The primary objectives are: (1) regression-based continuous risk score prediction, and (2) deriving binary loan approval classification from the predicted risk scores. The pipeline progresses through dataset exploration, categorical encoding, feature selection via Recursive Feature Elimination (RFE) using a Random Forest estimator, domain-informed feature engineering, standard scaling, and training of three progressively complex models: ElasticNet, a 1D Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), and TabNet—an attention-based tabular deep learning architecture. All models are trained on an 80/20 train-test split, with classification derived by applying a median threshold to the continuous risk score predictions.

The core modelling strategy employs a unified regression-to-classification approach: rather than building separate models for each task, a single regressor predicts the continuous risk score, and a threshold-based decision boundary converts these predictions into binary loan approval outcomes. Six engineered features—Income Stability Ratio, NetWorth-to-Income Ratio, Debt Stress Index, Credit Experience Score, Historical Risk Flag, and Composite Risk Index—augment the ten RFE-selected base features before scaling. Each model is evaluated using MAE, MSE, RMSE, R² Score, Explained Variance, Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-Score, and ROC-AUC. TabNet achieves the best performance across both tasks with MAE of 1.8953, R² of 0.8839, classification accuracy of 89.1%, and ROC-AUC of 0.9598.

The pipeline concludes with a Streamlit web application that loads the saved TabNet model and scaler, applies live feature engineering on user inputs, generates a risk score, applies a business rule governance layer (threshold greater than 50 triggers rejection), and calls the OpenAI GPT-4o-mini API to produce a formal underwriting report including risk interpretation, governance analysis, financial strengths, risk drivers, and executive summary. This integration of deep learning inference with LLM-based explanation bridges the gap between model output and human-centric decision communication.
