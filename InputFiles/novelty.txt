The research introduces a strategically layered and methodologically cohesive pipeline for automated resume classification that addresses three underexplored dimensions simultaneously: integrated multi-model benchmarking, systematic class imbalance mitigation, and deployment-ready entity extraction. Unlike prior studies that evaluate either traditional or transformer-based models in isolation, this work constructs a unified experimental framework comparing Multinomial Naive Bayes, Bidirectional LSTM, and DistilBERT under identical preprocessing, feature engineering, and evaluation conditions — enabling reproducible, apples-to-apples performance attribution. A particularly distinctive contribution is the custom geometric mean metric, which exposes class imbalance pathologies invisible to accuracy and macro F1 alone, revealing that baseline Bi-LSTM's G-Mean of 0.0910 and MNB's 0.0792 mask catastrophic failure on minority categories despite acceptable macro averages. The imbalance correction strategy itself represents an architectural innovation: rather than relying on a single remediation technique, the pipeline applies three complementary mechanisms — sample-weighted training, SMOTE oversampling, and per-class softmax threshold tuning — and evaluates their differential effects per model family, revealing that class weighting degrades Bi-LSTM (G-Mean collapse from 0.0910 to 0.0158) while dramatically benefiting DistilBERT (G-Mean rise to 0.7776). The per-class threshold optimisation procedure — scanning probability thresholds per class from 0.05 to 0.90 and selecting by maximum binary F1 — is a post-hoc calibration mechanism seldom applied in resume classification literature. Furthermore, the pipeline uniquely couples document-level classification with span-level Named Entity Recognition via a custom spaCy PhraseMatcher vocabulary covering 31 skills, 17 education qualifications, and 15 professional roles, converting unstructured resume text into structured HR-actionable outputs. The complete pipeline is operationalised in a Flask web service supporting real-time PDF resume processing through PyMuPDF, bridging the gap between research prototype and deployable HR automation software.
