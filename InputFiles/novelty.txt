The primary novelty of this research lies in the systematic integration of a structured multi-step preprocessing pipeline with a modern transformer-inspired convolutional architecture — ConvNeXt-Tiny — evaluated under rigorous lesion-level data partitioning, an engineering combination not previously formalised in the dermatoscopic classification literature. Unlike prior work that applies preprocessing steps in isolation, this pipeline sequentially chains DullRazor morphological hair removal, per-channel colour normalisation, and CLAHE contrast enhancement before any model-level augmentation, ensuring that downstream augmentation operates on artefact-reduced images rather than raw acquisitions. This ordering is architecturally significant: by decoupling noise reduction from statistical augmentation, the pipeline prevents augmentation from reinforcing acquisition artefacts during training. At the architectural level, the ConvNeXt-Tiny classifier head deviates from the conventional global-average-pooling-to-linear design by inserting a LayerNorm layer between the backbone output and the projection linear layer — a design choice borrowed from language model normalisation strategies that stabilises gradient flow in fine-tuned convolutional networks operating on domain-shifted medical images. The addition of a 512-dimensional intermediate projection with ReLU and 0.5 dropout before the seven-class softmax layer provides a regularised low-rank bottleneck that implicitly compresses backbone representations toward diagnostically relevant subspaces, reducing overfitting on minority classes such as df and vasc. The use of lesion-ID-based train-validation-test splitting combined with WeightedRandomSampler — rather than the more common image-level stratification — addresses both data leakage and class imbalance simultaneously within the dataloader itself, avoiding the need for post-hoc correction. Collectively, these engineering decisions yield a 10.76 percentage-point improvement in classification accuracy over InceptionV3 and a 6.07-point gain in melanoma recall over Xception, with only marginal additional training time of under two minutes, representing a favourable efficiency-to-accuracy trade-off demonstrating that principled systems engineering, rather than architectural novelty alone, is sufficient to achieve clinically meaningful performance gains.
