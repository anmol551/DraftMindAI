The web application was tested across five representative job categories — Arts, Automobile, Advocate, Data Science IT, and HR — using sample PDF resumes uploaded through the UI. For Arts, the model correctly predicted the category with high confidence alongside extracted skills including communication and leadership. For Automobile, the prediction correctly identified the role with skills such as project management and technical. The Advocate category was correctly classified with education entities including bachelor and master detected by the NER module. For Data Science IT, the model correctly predicted Information-Technology with Python, SQL, machine learning, and data analysis extracted as top skills along with relevant roles such as data scientist and software engineer. For HR, the model predicted HR accurately with leadership, communication, and project management as dominant skills and hr manager as the extracted role. The confidence scores varied across categories ranging from moderate to high, and the probability distributions showed clear separation for well-represented categories. Minority categories with fewer training samples showed lower confidence, consistent with the class imbalance observed during training. All test resumes were processed successfully with no errors and appropriate JSON responses returned.
