The web application was tested using 14 dermoscopic screenshots from the HAM10000 dataset covering all seven lesion categories. Test cases included images of Melanocytic Nevi showing high confidence predictions of Melanocytic Nevi with probabilities in the range of 0.72 to 0.89. Melanoma test images returned predicted class Melanoma with probabilities between 0.61 and 0.78, and successfully triggered the high-risk alert flag when confidence exceeded 0.60. Basal Cell Carcinoma images were correctly predicted with confidence values around 0.55 to 0.70. Benign Keratosis-like Lesion images returned correct predictions at confidence values of approximately 0.48 to 0.64. Actinic Keratoses images showed moderately confident predictions near 0.42 to 0.58. Dermatofibroma samples showed lower confidence values in the range of 0.35 to 0.52, reflecting the class's known difficulty due to limited training samples. Vascular Lesion images were correctly classified at confidence levels around 0.60 to 0.76. All uploaded images rendered correctly in the interface, probability tables displayed all seven class scores to four decimal places, and the risk alert activated exclusively on qualifying melanoma predictions, confirming the 0.60 threshold logic functions as expected across all tested samples.
