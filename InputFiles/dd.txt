The HAM10000 ('Human Against Machine with 10000 training images') dataset consists of 10,015 dermatoscopic images collected from different populations, acquired and stored by different modalities. Cases include seven diagnostic categories: Actinic keratoses and intraepithelial carcinoma/Bowen's disease (akiec), basal cell carcinoma (bcc), benign keratosis-like lesions (bkl), dermatofibroma (df), melanoma (mel), melanocytic nevi (nv), and vascular lesions (vasc). Ground truth is confirmed through histopathology (>50%), follow-up examination, expert consensus, or in-vivo confocal microscopy. Images are 600×450 pixels saved in JPEG format. The dataset is highly class-imbalanced; nv is the dominant class (6,705 images) while df is the smallest (115 images). The dataset is sourced from Kaggle (https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/kmader/skin-cancer-mnist-ham10000).
