**Paper:** 'Enhanced Skin Cancer Diagnosis Using Optimized CNN Architecture and Checkpoints for Automated Dermatological Lesion Classification' (BMC Medical Imaging, 2024). **Pipeline:** The research employs a comprehensive pipeline starting with Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the HAM10000 dataset. Preprocessing involves image resizing, pixel normalisation, and class-imbalance mitigation through targeted data augmentation (rotation, flipping, zooming). A custom CNN architecture with multiple convolutional, pooling, and dense layers is designed specifically for seven-class skin lesion classification. Model checkpointing is used to save best-performing weights during training, and training-validation splits are applied to monitor overfitting. **Dataset:** HAM10000 — 10,015 dermatoscopic images across seven classes: akiec, bcc, bkl, df, mel, nv, and vasc, with confirmed ground truth via histopathology, expert consensus, or confocal microscopy. Class imbalance is addressed through augmentation. **Algorithm Accuracy:** The study compares multiple CNN configurations. Traditional ML classifiers (SVM, KNN, Random Forest) report accuracies in the 55–72% range on lesion features. The custom CNN without augmentation achieves approximately 71%. The optimised CNN with augmentation and checkpointing achieves the best accuracy of approximately 74.2%. **Best Algorithm:** The optimized CNN with data augmentation and checkpoint-based model selection achieves the highest accuracy at approximately 74.2%, outperforming baseline CNNs and classical ML approaches on the seven-class classification task. **Novel Element:** The novelty lies in combining an application-specific custom CNN architecture with a systematic checkpointing strategy that preserves the best validation weights, paired with targeted augmentation addressing class imbalance without external generative models. **Future Recommendations:** The paper recommends integration of transformer-based or attention-enhanced architectures, use of larger and more diverse multi-institution datasets, exploration of federated learning for privacy-preserving training, and development of real-time clinical deployment pipelines with explainability modules (e.g., Grad-CAM). **Reference:** Adebiyi, A., Abdalnabi, N., Hoffman, E.S., Hirner, J., Simoes, E., Becevic, M. and Rao, P.
