**Topic Overview:** Automated dermatoscopic image classification for melanoma screening is a critical challenge in medical AI, where variability in lesion appearance, lighting, and acquisition settings creates significant domain shift. Large benchmark datasets such as HAM10000 and SIIM-ISIC challenge corpora have advanced the field but expose persistent class imbalance and patient-level data leakage issues (Cassidy, 2022; Kurtansky et al., 2024). **Importance of the Field:** Melanoma is among the deadliest skin cancers when detected late, yet dermoscopy-based triage can improve early detection rates substantially. Automated classification reduces clinician burden and enables screening in underserved areas. Computational tools evaluated on histopathology-confirmed test sets are increasingly recognised as viable clinical decision-support instruments (Naqvi et al., 2023; Ray et al., 2024). **Key Technical Content:** Preprocessing is a core pipeline component. DullRazor remains widely used for hair removal (Kasmi et al., 2023), while deep learning-based inpainting offers superior quality at higher computational cost (Delibasis et al., 2023). GAN-based colour normalisation (DermoCC-GAN) improved multiclass accuracy by several percentage points (Branciforti et al., 2023). CLAHE and white-balancing reduce inter-device variance (Salvi et al., 2024). Architecturally, ConvNeXt (Liu et al., 2022) achieved ImageNet top-1 ~87.8%, matching transformers in natural image tasks and demonstrating strong transferability to dermatoscopic classification when fine-tuned with class-balanced sampling (Ray et al., 2024; Himel et al., 2024). Ensemble strategies combining DenseNet121, Xception, and EfficientNetB0 achieved AUC ~0.96 on curated splits (Naqvi et al., 2023). Transformer and Swin-based hybrids capture long-range context but offer marginal gains over carefully tuned CNNs at higher compute cost (Gulzar et al., 2022; Sarker, 2022). **Findings and Insights:** Transfer learning from ImageNet-pretrained weights with gradual unfreezing consistently outperforms training from scratch. Clinical covariates (age, lesion site) fused with image features improve melanoma AUC (Himel et al., 2024). Patient-level splits yield lower but more realistic metrics than image-level cross-validation (Cassidy, 2022). Sensitivity for melanoma at a fixed false-positive rate is the clinically meaningful evaluation criterion (Ray et al., 2024). **Limitations Identified:** Dataset bias toward lighter skin tones, inconsistent annotation quality, and absence of prospective clinical validation limit translation. Image-level evaluation inflates reported performance due to patient-level leakage (Cassidy, 2022). Preprocessing pipelines are rarely standardised across studies. **Unresolved Issues:** Generalisation across datasets and acquisition devices remains unproven. Fairness across skin tones is unaddressed. Interpretability and model calibration for clinical deployment are insufficiently studied. ConvNeXt's behaviour under different fine-tuning strategies in dermatoscopy remains under-explored (Ray et al., 2024).
