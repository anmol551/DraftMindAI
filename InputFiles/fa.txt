Iterative model development and design exploration inevitably involved approaches that underperformed before arriving at the final optimised pipeline, offering important lessons in architectural and methodological decision-making.

**Training from Scratch (No Transfer Learning):** An early design iteration attempted to train InceptionV3 and a simple CNN without ImageNet-pretrained weights. Without pretrained feature representations, models failed to converge meaningfully within 20 epochs on the relatively small HAM10000 training set, producing accuracy values below 50% and heavily biased toward the dominant nv class. The absence of pretrained inductive biases made it computationally infeasible to learn discriminative dermatoscopic features from limited data, confirming that transfer learning is non-negotiable for this domain.

**Image-Level Train-Test Split:** Initial experiments used image-level stratified random splitting rather than lesion-ID-based partitioning. This approach introduced patient-level data leakage, as multiple images of the same lesion appeared in both training and test sets, artificially inflating accuracy estimates by 3–5 percentage points. Upon discovering the redundant lesion images tracked by lesion_id in the metadata, the split strategy was corrected to lesion-level partitioning, producing more realistic and lower — but clinically meaningful — performance estimates.

**Single-Step Preprocessing:** Early iterations applied only CLAHE without DullRazor hair removal or colour normalisation, resulting in the model learning spurious hair-texture features correlated with lesion labels rather than genuine pigmentation patterns. This produced high precision on the majority class but degraded melanoma recall, confirming that sequential multi-step preprocessing is necessary rather than optional. Applying preprocessing steps in a non-sequential or partial manner yielded visually distorted images when CLAHE was applied before hair removal, generating high-contrast artefacts around hair strands that confused the convolutional feature extractor.
