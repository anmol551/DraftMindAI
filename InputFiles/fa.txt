Failed Attempts and Design Iterations

The development process involved several unsuccessful or suboptimal strategies that informed the final pipeline design through empirical failure analysis.

1. Class-Weighted Bi-LSTM (G-Mean Degradation):
Applying class-weighted CrossEntropyLoss to the Bi-LSTM model paradoxically degraded performance, with G-Mean collapsing from 0.0910 to 0.0158 and accuracy dropping from 0.7042 to 0.6881. The failure is attributable to the interaction between class weighting and the Adam optimiser's adaptive gradient scaling — high weights on minority classes produced large gradient updates that destabilised the LSTM's hidden state dynamics, causing catastrophic interference in the learned sequential representations. Unlike transformers, which have layer normalisation and attention mechanisms that stabilise gradient flow, recurrent networks are more vulnerable to loss landscape distortion caused by extreme class weight imbalances.

2. SMOTE on TF-IDF Dense Matrix (Marginal Gains):
SMOTE oversampling applied to the dense TF-IDF feature matrix improved G-Mean to 0.2025 but underperformed compared to weighted MNB (G-Mean 0.2091) despite generating synthetic samples for all minority classes. The failure stems from TF-IDF's high-dimensional, sparse feature space — synthetic interpolation between minority-class vectors in this space does not reliably produce semantically valid resume representations, as linear interpolation of term-frequency scores creates artificial documents with feature distributions that do not correspond to real resume language patterns.

3. Baseline DistilBERT Without Imbalance Correction (Poor G-Mean):
The baseline DistilBERT model achieved strong accuracy (0.8189) but a G-Mean of only 0.2990, indicating continued majority-class dominance despite the model's contextual power. This demonstrated that transformer capability alone cannot overcome dataset imbalance without deliberate loss function modification or post-hoc threshold calibration, motivating the weighted fine-tuning and threshold-tuning strategies adopted in the final pipeline.
